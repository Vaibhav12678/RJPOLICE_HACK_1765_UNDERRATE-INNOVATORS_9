Matrimonial Fraud

Matrimonial fraud, also known as marriage fraud or romance scam, involves deceptive practices in the context of relationships or marriage.
Too Good to Be True:
Be cautious if the person seems too perfect or if the relationship progresses very quickly. Scammers often create idealized personas to gain trust.
Requests for Money:
A common tactic is for the scammer to invent a crisis and request financial assistance. Be wary of requests for money, especially if you have never met the person in real life.
Avoids Face-to-Face Meetings:
If the person consistently avoids in-person meetings or uses excuses to postpone them, it might be a red flag.
Inconsistent Stories:
Scammers may provide inconsistent information about their background, job, or personal details. Pay attention to any discrepancies in their stories.
Refusal to Share Personal Information:
A reluctance to share personal information or provide contact details may indicate that the person is not genuine.
Unusual Behavior Online:
Be cautious if the person is reluctant to video chat or always has reasons for not being able to talk on the phone.
Isolation from Friends and Family:
Scammers may try to isolate their victims from friends and family to maintain control over the situation.

Steps to Protect Yourself:



Be Skeptical:
Approach online relationships with a healthy dose of skepticism. If something feels off or too good to be true, investigate further.
Verify Information:
Conduct online searches to verify the person's identity and cross-check the information they provide.
Do Not Share Financial Information:
Never share your financial information or send money to someone you've only met online, regardless of the reasons they give.
Meet in Person:
If you're in an online relationship, make efforts to meet in person as soon as it's reasonable and safe to do so.
Share with Trusted Individuals:
Discuss your online relationship with friends or family members and get their perspective. They may notice red flags that you missed.
Report Suspicious Activity:
If you suspect fraud, report the individual to the platform where you met them and to relevant authorities.
Educate Yourself:
Stay informed about common romance scams and online fraud tactics. Awareness is crucial for prevention.
Trust Your Instincts:
If something doesn't feel right, trust your instincts and take the necessary steps to protect yourself.
