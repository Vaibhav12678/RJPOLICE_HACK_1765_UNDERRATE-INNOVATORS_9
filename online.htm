Online Shopping Fraud

Online shopping fraud occurs when individuals or entities engage in deceptive practices to exploit consumers during online shopping transactions.
Fake Online Stores:
Scammers create fraudulent websites that mimic legitimate online stores, enticing users to make purchases but never delivering the products.
Phishing Scams:
Emails, messages, or ads that appear to be from reputable online retailers trick users into providing personal or financial information.
Non-Delivery Scams:
Sellers receive payment but never deliver the purchased items. This can happen on legitimate platforms or through fake online stores.
Counterfeit Products:
Fraudsters sell fake or counterfeit goods, misrepresenting them as genuine products from reputable brands.
Account Takeover:
Hackers gain unauthorized access to users' online shopping accounts, make unauthorized purchases, or steal personal information.
Overpayment Scams:
Fraudsters posing as buyers overpay for products and then request a refund of the excess amount, often using fake or stolen payment details.
